# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Business owners and operators who have outgrown their current setup but have not yet hired a technical team. They run local service businesses (cleaning, junk removal, staffing, insurance), growing mid-market companies, or multi-location operations. They are not technical, but they are sharp about their business. They arrive stressed: forms going unanswered, admin eating their team's week, tools that don't talk to each other. They have probably been burned by a vendor who overpromised and underdelivered.

Secondary users: CTOs and ops leads at larger companies shopping for a delivery partner, not a vendor.

## Product Purpose

Oblique Path builds AI automation systems, custom software platforms, mobile apps, and voice agents for businesses done running manually. The site exists to convert qualified visitors into discovery calls. Success is a booked call, not a scroll session.

## Brand Personality

Premium, calm, trustworthy.

The agency that does not need to sell hard because the work speaks. Unhurried. Specific. No buzzwords, no hype, no AI imagery. Expensive-feeling without being cold.

Voice: direct and plain. Talks to operators, not developers. Uses real numbers. Finishes sentences. Does not hedge.

## Anti-references

All four quadrants are off-limits:

- Generic SaaS purple (Intercom/Notion clones: soft gradients, hero metrics, rounded everything)
- AI hype aesthetic (glowing orbs, particle fields, neon on dark, robot imagery, "powered by" badges)
- Corporate IT consultant (stock handshakes, blue gradient hero, enterprise-bro energy, suits)
- Cheap agency template (Canva-tier, icon-box grids, alternating feature rows, lorem ipsum energy)

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** An automation agency's site should itself feel precise and frictionless. No wasted motion, no unnecessary fields, no copy that makes the visitor work.
2. **Specificity over promises.** Real numbers, real client names, real timelines. "70% fewer no-shows" beats "transform your workflow" every time.
3. **Understand before you pitch.** Lead with the problem the visitor has, not the solution being sold. The visitor should feel recognized before they feel sold to.
4. **Calm authority.** The confidence of someone who does not need to shout. Restraint is the signal that communicates premium.
5. **Every element earns its place.** If removing something would not hurt the page, remove it. Density is not depth.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Reduced motion: Framer Motion animations should respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Color contrast: muted-foreground text must pass 4.5:1 against background. No information conveyed by color alone.
